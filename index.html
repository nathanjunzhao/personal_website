Projects

______________________

The Calgary Science Spelling Challenge (CSSC) is an organization based in my hometown in Calgary, Canada that worked to encourage scientific exploration in youth through vocabulary and teamwork during a science-themed team-based annual spelling bee we hosted. Before leaving for university, I was the Vice President of the organization and fully in-charge of the technological side of the event: the website, scoring, and tabulation.
Website

BundleUp was a temporary business made under the Junior Achievement (JA) program. The business strived to make the adjacent street to our school a more accessible source of food, snacks, and merchandise for our students. More importantly, it aimed to re-ignite the stagnated passion of the street that the Covid-19 pandemic caused through incentivising student engagement in the street's stores. I was the Vice President of IT in this organization and I designed the coupon delivery system of our business that served as our primary product to provide discounts to students who purchased our "Bundles" of coupons.
Website

As a member of my highschool "Leadership" program I engaged heavily in the athletic community in a variety of ways. Outside of physical engagement, I also helped manage the athletic website, team photos on the website, updates and announcements, as well as the website's design and pages. Additionally, under the volleyball team I collected statistics and game-footage for players and coaches. In this way, I was both a technician and statistician for the athletic community in my highschool, as well as an athlete.
